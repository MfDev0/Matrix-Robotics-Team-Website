V2 Concept

In the second version of the robot (V2), the main focus was on precisely arranging the balls inside the robot according to the game requirements, aiming to increase the chances of achieving Ranking Points.

In the first version, we faced issues with ball instability inside the system, leading to inaccurate feeding and inconsistent performance. Therefore, in V2 we focused on:

  • Embracing balls and preventing random movement
  • Organizing ball arrangement before launch
  • Improving ball transfer path inside the robot
  • Ensuring consistent and repeated shooter feeding

The goal was not just to launch the balls, but to launch them in the correct order and at the right time.

Intake System

Design
  • Flexible sticks to increase intake range
  • 3D printed intake unit (first stage)
  • 6 rollers powered by a single motor
  • Improving intake efficiency and reducing failure
Challenges
  • Need for precise balance between flexibility and strength
  • Ball may not enter smoothly if angle is not suitable

Spindexer System

V1 Problems
  • Poor ball embracing
  • Random movement inside robot
  • Unstable ball position before launch
  • Inaccurate shooter feeding
V2 Improvements
  • Design that embraces balls and prevents movement
  • Servo rotates balls at 120° angle
  • Directing each ball to the launch position in an organized manner

// MECHANISM_C

Shooter System

Design & Components
  • Turret system allows 360° rotation
  • Gear system: Driver 30 - Driven 100
  • 3D printed connector + speed gear
  • Advanced ball guide plates
Challenges
  • Shooter vibration due to imprecise flywheel mounting
  • Slight loss in RPM due to using gears and chain instead of direct drive
